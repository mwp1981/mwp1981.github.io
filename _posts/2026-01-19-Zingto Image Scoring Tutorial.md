# Zingto Image Scoring Tutorial: AI-Powered Photo Selection with Free Stock Images
Do you ever feel overwhelmed by the sheer number of photos you’ve taken, struggling to choose just a few to share on social media? Does endless scrolling leave you indecisive and unsure which shots are truly your best? **Zingto Image Scoring** is here to help—offering a smart, efficient way to evaluate your images and take the guesswork out of selecting the finest ones. This AI-driven **image rating tool** simplifies photo culling for photographers, content creators, and casual users alike.

## About Zingto AI Image Scoring Model
The Zingto AI Image Scoring general-purpose model is highly versatile, supporting multiple photography categories including **landscapes, portraits, animals, architecture, and still life**. 

## Tutorial: Batch Photo Scoring with Zingto (Using Pixabay Free Images)
In this tutorial, we use free, copyright-free stock images from **Pixabay** (a popular free stock photo platform) for demonstration. Follow these steps to batch-score and filter high-quality images with Zingto:

### Step 1: Prepare Free Stock Images
In this tutorial, we use the free image library from Pixabay for demonstration. First, filter images with pixels larger than 800×600, published within the last 7 days, and exclude AI-generated ones, resulting in 286 free stock photos. Then, download them to your local computer using a batch download tool.

### Step 2: Import Images to Zingto
Run the Zingto Image Scoring software, click the **“Open Image Folder”** button, and select the folder containing the 286 downloaded Pixabay images. The software will automatically load all images for analysis.

### Step 3: Batch AI Scoring
The Zingto scoring model will start batch analysis immediately—no manual intervention required. It automatically assigns a score (typically 0-10) to each image based on comprehensive quality metrics.
The batch analysis runs in the background, and you’ll see scores populate next to each image once completed. This process is optimized for speed, even with hundreds of high-resolution images.

### Step 4: Sort and Manage Images
Navigate to the **“Image List”** section. Right-click to open the context menu, where you can:
- Sort images by score (descending/ascending) to prioritize top-quality shots
- Sort by image name for organized file management

Select unwanted category images and click the **“Delete Selected”** button to remove them from the working list.

### Step 5: Filter High-Score Images
The score filter has a default threshold of 6.5. Adjust this value to refine your selection: here, we set it to 7.9 to retain only the top 20 highest-rated images. Once filtered, right-click the list and choose **“Save Images As”** to store the high-quality pictures in a new folder for easy access.
